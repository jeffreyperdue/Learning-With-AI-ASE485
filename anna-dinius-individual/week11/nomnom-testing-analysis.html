# Combination & Methods Timing Why This Combination? How It Applies to NomNom Safe
1
Core Qualitative Loop
Moderated Usability Testing User Interviews
Weeks 1–2
Feasibility
4/5

Moderated usability testing is the gold standard for MVP-stage apps, especially when testing complex, multi-step workflows where users may get stuck. Pairing it with a brief pre- or post-task interview transforms a task observation into a full insight session. Together they answer both what users do and why they do it.

The combination is feasible remotely via Zoom or Google Meet — eliminating the in-person scheduling constraint entirely — and with just 5 participants it covers ~85% of usability issues per the Nielsen Norman Group benchmark.

Claude MCP use: Generate task scripts, scenario prompts, and interview question banks ahead of sessions. MCP can also help draft a consent/recording release template.

The business side of NomNom Safe has several multi-step workflows that are critical to validate: onboarding (setup wizard Steps 1–3 in SetUp/), adding/editing menu items (AddMenuItem, MenuItemPanel), and managing business info (EditBusinessInfo). Restaurant owners — especially non-technical ones — may find these flows confusing without guidance, making moderated observation essential.

Post-task interviews can surface domain-specific expectations, e.g., how owners think about allergen assignment or whether the "one menu per restaurant" constraint matches their mental model. This directly informs whether the current data model (one menu_id per restaurant) causes friction.
2
Async Validation Pass
Unmoderated Remote Testing Surveys & Feedback Forms
Week 2–3
5/5

Business owners are busy — unmoderated testing respects their schedule by letting them complete tasks at their own pace without a live facilitator. This drastically reduces the coordination burden given your 3-week constraint. Appending a short post-session survey (e.g., System Usability Scale or a custom 5-question form) converts each session into both behavioral and attitudinal data.

This combination scales well: if Combination 1 reveals a specific pain point, you can deploy a targeted unmoderated task to 3–5 additional participants quickly to validate whether the fix resolved it. The survey captures satisfaction scores you can track across iterations.

Claude MCP use: Draft the full unmoderated task script and the post-session survey. MCP can also help format SUS scoring rubrics or generate follow-up questions conditioned on task success/failure.

Once the major flows are confirmed stable after Combination 1 sessions, unmoderated testing is ideal for validating specific MVP features: sign-in/sign-up (SignInUp), the allergen assignment UI in AllergenList, and the menu item editor. These are well-defined, self-contained tasks that don't require real-time facilitation.

The post-session survey can measure whether owners understand the allergen model (preset allergen IDs from the Firestore schema) and whether they find the single-menu constraint acceptable for their business context — both critical product-market fit signals.
3
Pre-Session Expert Sweep
Heuristic Evaluation AI-Driven Synthetic Testing
Week 1 · Before Users
5/5

Running a heuristic evaluation before involving real users filters out obvious, fixable issues so that your limited participant pool (food-service business owners are hard to recruit) is not wasted on surface-level problems. Nielsen's 10 heuristics can be applied directly to each screen without scheduling anyone.

AI-driven synthetic testing (e.g., Uxia or Lighthouse-based automated checks) complements this by catching WCAG accessibility violations and predicted click-confusion zones instantly. Together, this two-pronged sweep costs almost nothing in time and protects the quality of your live user sessions.

Claude MCP use: Generate a heuristic evaluation checklist pre-populated with NomNom Safe's specific screens and flows. Claude can also produce a structured severity-rating rubric for triaging findings before user testing begins.

The business-side MVP has numerous forms and modals: ChangeEmail, ChangePassword, EditLoginInfo, EditBusinessInfo, and the multi-step SetUp wizard. A heuristic pass will quickly surface consistency issues (e.g., error message formatting across ErrorMessage vs. ConfirmationMessage components) and clarity problems before real owners encounter them.

Automated accessibility checks are especially relevant given that NomNom Safe serves food-allergy use cases — the business owners setting allergen data have a direct impact on vulnerable end-users, so the input UI must be unambiguous and accessible.
4
Lightweight Behavioral Tail
Heatmaps & Session Recording Analytics-Driven Testing
Week 2–3 · Passive
4/5

These methods require no scheduling, no facilitation, and no participant coordination — they run passively once configured. Hotjar or Microsoft Clarity (free tier) can be installed in under an hour and immediately begin collecting click maps and session replays across all unmoderated and beta sessions.

The combination provides the quantitative counterpart to the qualitative insight from Combinations 1 and 2, answering "how often does this happen" rather than just "why." This is especially useful for validating fixes: after resolving a usability issue found in Combination 1, heatmaps confirm whether behavior actually changed.

Claude MCP use: After collecting session data, use Claude to help interpret heatmap patterns or summarize session replay notes — keeping AI in the setup/analysis layer, not the testing execution layer.

Given the MVP's dashboard-style layout (MenuDashboard, MenuCard, AdminTable), heatmaps can reveal whether business owners are finding the key CTAs (e.g., "Add Menu Item," "Edit Business Info") or being drawn to visually prominent but less important elements. Session recordings of the ChooseBusiness / SetUp flow are especially valuable for spotting where owners abandon the onboarding sequence.

Analytics drop-off tracking through the multi-step setup flow (Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3) will quantify exactly which step causes the most abandonment — a concrete, prioritizable finding for the remaining sprint.
Recommended execution order: (1) Run the heuristic + AI sweep in Week 1 and patch obvious issues. (2) Conduct 3–5 moderated remote sessions with interviews in Weeks 1–2, using Claude MCP to prepare scripts. (3) Deploy unmoderated tasks + surveys in Week 2–3 as a wider validation pass. (4) Let heatmaps and session recordings run passively throughout, reviewing findings at the end of Week 3.

Note on methods excluded: A/B testing (requires significant traffic), Card/Tree Sorting (IA is largely fixed at MVP stage), Prototype Testing (already in a live build), Contextual Inquiry (in-person field requirement), Guerrilla Testing (non-target users), and Beta Testing (requires a feature-complete stable build and support infrastructure) were omitted as incompatible with the timeline, user profile, or current app stage.
