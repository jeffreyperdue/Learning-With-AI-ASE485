# 6-Week Roadmap for Market Validation & User-Focused Research

> Generated using Perplexity

## Overview

This roadmap breaks down learning Market Validation and User-Focused Research into actionable steps for your NomNom Safe app over 6 weeks. Each week fits 1-2 hours, focusing on quick learning, testing with users, gathering feedback, and iterating to improve your app.

## Week 1: Learn Basics (1 hour)

Start with core concepts of market validation and user research. Read summaries on methods like user interviews, surveys, and problem validation to identify if NomNom Safe solves real pain points for your target users (e.g., food safety concerns). Create a simple problem statement: "Parents struggle to check if food is safe for kids." [vldt](https://www.vldt.ai/blog/how-founders-validate-new-apps)

Spend 30 minutes defining 1-2 user personas based on demographics and needs.

## Week 2: Plan Research (1 hour)

Choose 2-3 low-effort methods suited for apps: short surveys and user interviews. Draft 5-10 open-ended questions about users' current food safety habits and pain points (e.g., "What frustrates you most about checking food ingredients?"). [designmonks](https://www.designmonks.co/blog/user-centered-research-methods)

Identify 5-10 potential testers from your network or local Florence, KY parenting groups.

## Week 3: Run Initial Tests (1.5 hours)

Conduct 3-5 quick user interviews (10-15 mins each) or send a 5-question survey via free tools like Google Forms. Focus on validating demand: Do users want NomNom Safe's features? [zigpoll](https://www.zigpoll.com/content/what-are-some-effective-market-validation-techniques-for-testing-user-engagement-and-demand-for-a-new-app-feature-before-fullscale-development)

Log feedback in a simple table: pain points, feature likes/dislikes, willingness to use.

## Week 4: Analyze & Prioritize (1 hour)

Review feedback to spot patterns (e.g., top 3 issues users face). Prioritize 1-2 app improvements, like better scanning UI based on usability insights. [nngroup](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/which-ux-research-methods/)

Sketch quick wireframes of changes using paper or free tools like Figma.

## Week 5: Test Iterations (1.5 hours)

Share updated wireframes or a basic prototype with the same 5 testers for usability feedback. Use tasks like "Scan this food item and rate ease." [interaction-design](https://www.interaction-design.org/literature/article/getting-into-the-mobile-app-user-s-mind-user-research-for-mobile-applications)

Collect responses and note metrics: success rate, time taken, satisfaction scores.

## Week 6: Iterate & Document (1 hour)

Synthesize all data into key learnings (e.g., "Users need faster scans; validated via 80% positive feedback"). Plan next tests or full prototype. [vldt](https://www.vldt.ai/blog/how-founders-validate-new-apps)

Write a 1-page report for class: methods used, results, improvements for NomNom Safe.
